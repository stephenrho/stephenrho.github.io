Current Projects

Tablet Assessment of Short-Term Memory Binding

Research assistant on MRC Confidence in Concept (CiC) grant (awarded to Profs. Sergio Della Sala, Robert Logie and Dr Mario Parra) evaluating a tablet version of the short-term memory binding task. Previous versions of this task have been shown to be sensitive to both sporadic and familial Alzheimer’s disease. This project will assess the sensitivity and specificity of the portable tablet version with a group of patients with mild-AD and a group of healthy controls.

Interaction Effects in Recognition

In recognition experiments it is important to separate out the contribution of an observer’s sensitivity from their bias towards a specific response (e.g. a tendency to say same). To do this one has to make some assumptions regarding the process of recognition or the information available to the observer. Popular measures include those that assume a continuous process with a `signal’ compared to a criterion (d-prime) or those that assume a small number of discrete states (hits minus false-alarms). The choice between measures can greatly affect conclusions regarding differences between groups or conditions with differing bias (as shown here and here). I am working on simulations assessing the effect that choice of measurement has on tests of interactions.

Informed Guessing in Change Detection

In change detection observers are presented with a set of items (e.g. colours) to remember and following a brief delay these items are re-presented with the possibility that one item has changed. Assuming observers occasionally don’t have enough information to perform the judgment (i.e. some items were not encoded or retained) can they use knowledge of the number of items they have in memory and the number of items presented to inform their guessing? Only one or two studies have assessed this proposal.
